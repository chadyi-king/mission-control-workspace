# RE:UNITE Style Guide

## Writing Style Overview

**Target Tone:**
- 70% serious/strategic/political
- 30% comedy/family warmth
- Never fully grimdark, never fully silly
- Balance between action and character moments

**Narrative Style:**
- Third person limited (Ryfel's perspective)
- Heavy internal monologue from Ryfel
- Tone shifts between serious political/strategic and comedy/family moments

---

## Dialogue Format

### Standard Format:
```
Character (emotion/descriptor): "Dialogue text"
```

### Examples:
```
Ryker (grinning): "That's my boy!"
Kriscila (quiet, sincere): "Just... be careful, okay?"
Ryfel (thinking): *I need to learn this language. Fast.*
```

### Character Voice Guide

**Ryfel (Protagonist):**
- Precise, measured words
- Strategic questions
- Internal: SEAL references, political comparisons
- External: Respectful but independent
- Curses in English when frustrated (internal)

**Ryker (Father):**
- Exclamation-heavy
- Repeats names: "RYYY-KERRR!", "Little Ry!"
- Boisterous, loud
- Paternal pride in every word
- Farming metaphors

**Elda (Mother):**
- Measured, thoughtful
- Dry humor
- Sighs often
- Questions Ryker's dramatics
- Softens with genuine emotion

**Milal (Servant):**
- Casual, friendly
- "Young Master" for Ryfel
- Food references constantly
- Informal with family
- Exclaims about food

**Kriscila:**
- Public: Sharp, confrontational
- Private (with Ryfel): Stammers, softens
- Combat: Commands, observations
- Drunk: Giggling, loose

**Bram:**
- Casual, friendly
- Food references
- "He he heh" laughter
- Combat enthusiasm
- Teasing humor

**Dain:**
- Initially: Arrogant, mocking
- Later: Respectful, supportive
- Combat: Names attacks
- Masochistic grin descriptions

## Action Scene Style

### Pacing:
- Fast-paced but with strategic pauses
- Detailed sensory descriptions
- Magic described through physical sensation
- Strategic analysis during combat
- Sound effects in brackets: [CLANG!] [FWOOSH!] [BANG!]

### Combat Structure:
1. Setup/Assessment (Ryfel's OODA loop)
2. Initial engagement
3. Adaptation/Mid-fight strategy shift
4. Climax/Finisher
5. Aftermath/Respect shown

### Example:
```
Ryfel narrowed his eyes, studying the mirrors. A smirk tugged at his lips.

Ryfel (calm, confident): "I have studied you for a long time, Sovereign..."

He channeled light magic into the mirrors—suddenly, dozens of Ryfels appeared.

Kriscila (shocked): "He's using Aurex's own technique against him!"

[CLANG! CLANG! CLANG!]

The clones clashed, steel ringing across the arena.
```

---

## Magic System Exposition

### Show Don't Tell:
- Demonstrate through training scenes
- Ryfel learns by experimenting
- Family teaches through practical examples
- Avoid long infodumps

### Good Example (from text):
```
Ryker lifted the first cup, the one containing earth.

Ryker: "Earth is the most common and easiest to learn..."

With a motion, the soil floated up, swirling lazily.

Ryfel watched carefully. "He isn't 'commanding' it... He's manipulating the mana already present."
```

### Bad Example (avoid):
```
Earth magic works by channeling mana through your hands into the ground. It requires specific gestures and breath control. The earth element is patient but stubborn...
```

## Chapter Structure

### Standard Template:

**Opening:**
- Scene-setting (atmosphere, sensory)
- Character interaction/dialogue
- Establish current situation

**Middle:**
- Strategic revelation or training
- Character development
- Rising tension

**Climactic Moment:**
- Combat, discovery, or emotional beat
- Peak action/drama

**Closing Hook:**
- Sets up next chapter
- New threat, promise, or question
- Tournament advancement

### Chapter Length:
- Target: 10,000-30,000 words
- Longer for major events (tournament finals)
- Shorter for setup/transition chapters

## Internal Monologue

### Ryfel's Thought Patterns:

**SEAL Mode (Combat):**
- *OODA loop: Observe, Orient...*
- *Tactical advantage...*
- *That's no normal magic...*
- American/English curse words
- Strategic analysis

**Relaxed Mode (Family):**
- *This family is something else...*
- *Unacceptable. I used to wear the finest suits...*
- Amused observations
- Cultural comparisons

**Format:**
- Use *italics* for thoughts
- Or (Thinking) tag in dialogue
- Keep internal dialogue distinct from narrative

### Example:
```
Ryfel (thinking): *This isn't magic like in fantasy stories. This is... something else.*

But before he could process further—

Ryker suddenly grabbed him and tossed him playfully into the air.

Ryfel's entire soul left his body for a second.
```

## Comedy Elements

### Types of Humor:

**1. Family Dynamics:**
- Ryker's over-the-top enthusiasm
- Parents spinning celebration ritual
- Milal's food obsession
- Ryfel's internal frustration with baby body

**2. Cultural Misunderstandings:**
- "Magic Hoe" confusion (garden tool vs other meaning)
- Earth references others don't understand
- Ryfel's adult mind in child body

**3. Combat Humor:**
- Bram's ridiculous armor
- Dain naming attacks
- Opponent underestimating Ryfel

**4. Meta Humor:**
- References to American politics/pop culture (Ryfel's internal thoughts)
- "Make Our District Great Again" (political reference)

### Balance:
- Never undercut serious moments
- Comedy used for relief between intense scenes
- Family warmth offsets grim situations

---

## Emotional Beats

### Serious Moments:
- Dain's redemption and death
- Aurex's sacrifice for Lux
- Kriscila's confession
- Ryfel's capture
- Keep dialogue minimal, focus on action/reaction

### Romantic Moments:
- Build tension slowly
- Use physical descriptions (blushing, trembling)
- Kriscila needs liquid courage (ale)
- Ryfel's rare vulnerability

### Family Moments:
- Warm, sometimes chaotic
- Ryker's pride celebrations
- Elda's quiet concern
- Physical affection (hugs, ruffling hair)

---

## World Building Integration

### Through Action:
- Show magic in daily life (farmers using earth magic)
- District characteristics shown through characters
- Political dynamics in conversations

### Avoid:
- Long exposition dumps
- Encyclopedia entries
- Characters explaining what they already know

### Example of Good Integration:
```
Ryfel took in everything - carpenters moved planks with earth magic, bakers used fire magic for ovens, masons reinforced walls with stone magic.

Ryfel (thinking): *They live like this every day? Magic isn't just for combat...*
```

## Callbacks and References

### Target: 3-5 major callbacks per chapter

**Types:**
1. **SEAL Training References:**
   - "Say hello to my little friend" (Scarface)
   - OODA loop thinking
   - Tactical terminology
   - Modern military equipment (grenades, flashbangs)

2. **Family Callbacks:**
   - Spinning celebration ritual
   - "Little Ry" nickname evolution
   - Food references (honey-glazed eels)

3. **Political References:**
   - "Make Our District Great Again"
   - Don John Treagan (Trump parody)
   - Vice President background

4. **Magic System Callbacks:**
   - "Traps never fail" philosophy
   - Previous battle lessons applied
   - Training moments referenced

## Pacing Guidelines

### Action Scenes:
- Short paragraphs
- Quick dialogue exchanges
- Sound effects
- Minimal description during fast moments
- Slow down for key hits/finishes

### Character Scenes:
- Longer paragraphs
- Internal monologue
- Sensory details
- Emotional processing

### Transition Scenes:
- Establish time/place quickly
- Move plot forward efficiently
- End with hook

---

## Prohibited/Warning Elements

### Avoid:
- Over-explaining SEAL mindset (show through action)
- Breaking tone for extended periods
- Forgetting character voice distinctions
- Inconsistent magic system rules
- Making Ryfel too powerful without cost (mana depletion)

### Use Sparingly:
- Extended flashbacks
- Multiple POVs (stay with Ryfel)
- Romance overwhelming plot
- Grimdark without hope

---

## Chapter Ending Hooks

### Effective Types:

**1. New Threat:**
```
The wall exploded inward. Through the breach charged Knights, their violet fire casting death across the festival.
```

**2. Promise/Vow:**
```
Ryfel: "In three years, when I've become the leader I need to be... we can be together."
```

**3. Revelation:**
```
The King of Runevia smiled. "I finally found you."
```

**4. Capture/Danger:**
```
The chains clanked as they bound his arms. Ryfel's last thought before unconsciousness: *Runevia...*
```
